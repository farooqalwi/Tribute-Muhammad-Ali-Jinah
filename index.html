MUHAMMAD ALI JINNAH

Father of the nation and the founder of Pakistan

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was a barrister, politician and the founder of Pakistan. Jinnah served as the leader of the All-India Muslim League.

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali Jinah's life:

  • 1876 - Muhammed Ali Jinnah was born on 25 December 1876 in Karachi, now in Pakistan, but then part of British-controlled India. His father was a prosperous Muslim merchant.
  • 1913 - He joined the Muslim League. The league had formed a few years earlier to represent the interests of Indian Muslims in a predominantly Hindu country
  • 1916 - He was elected its president.
  • 1920 - The Indian National Congress launched a movement of non-cooperation to boycott all aspects of British rule. Jinnah opposed this policy and resigned from the congress. There were by now profound differences between the congress and the Muslim League.
  • 1937 - After provincial elections, the congress refused to form coalition administrations with the Muslim League in mixed areas.
  • 1940 - At a Muslim League session in Lahore, the first official demand was made for the partition of India and the creation of a Muslim state of Pakistan. Jinnah had always believed that Hindu-Muslim unity was possible, but reluctantly came to the view that partition was necessary to safeguard the rights of Indian Muslims.
  • 1947 - His insistence on this issue through negotiations with the British government resulted in the partition of India and the formation of the state of Pakistan on 14 August 1947. This occurred against a backdrop of widespread violence between Muslims, Hindus and Sikhs, and a vast movement of populations between the new states of Pakistan and India in which hundreds of thousands died.
  • 1948 - Jinnah became the first governor general of Pakistan, but died of tuberculosis on 11 September 1948.

Jinnah was an Indian politician who successfully campaigned for an independent Pakistan and became its first leader. He is known there as 'Quaid-I Azam' or 'Great Leader'.
